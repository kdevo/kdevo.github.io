About

I am a free-minded and passionate software engineer, tech enthusiast and maker, who never stops developing and evolving: You can call me kdevo, too.

By the start of 2021, I’ve finished my M.Eng. in Information Systems Engineering at the FH Aachen, where I took a deep dive into the emerging field of Reciprocal Recommender Systems from a research and practical point of view. Bringing these two views together, you can now read my published thesis or experiment with Chaos, a first of its kind framework for researching human-to-human recommendations.

Below you can find a variety of my work, coming to life when you interact with it. Lastly, feel free to connect with me on LinkedIn or reach out about anything.

Bachelor Thesis

About one year after my Bachelor graduation, I asked myself: Why shouldn’t other people have the possibility to read my thesis?

In my work, I analyzed how well the Ethereum Blockchain can be integrated into exisiting business processes of the FH Aachen. As a business process execution engine, I used the Open-Source Camunda BPM and wrote an own web3-connector to communicate with self-developed Smart Contracts.

Master Thesis

In my master’s thesis, I took a deep dive into the data science world and the emerging field of Reciprocal Recommender Systems (RRSs) which enable Human-to-Human recommendations.

I engineered Chaos, a first of its kind framework that aims to build a solid bridge between the research and development departments of RRSs.
